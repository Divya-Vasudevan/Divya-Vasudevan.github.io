Peppercorn Discovery Program

Ux Design + Information Architecture + Learning Platform

Designing a Peppercorn’s Discovery Platform that aids users in their job search process

My Role

Research, Information Architecture, UX/UI design


Duration

6 months

The Problem

Using Word and Excel documents for the program

Peppercorn had developed a job search program that would enable candidates to discover what they wanted for their future while helping them articulate themselves effectively to land the right job. Currently, the program consists of multiple Word and Excel documents. These documents contain instructions for the program and tasks to complete. The issues were that:

  • Using Word and Excel documents was complex requiring someone to walk the clients through the necessary steps.

  • The candidate has to do work that could be made easier with a system that uses a database.

  • Large documents filled with content can be intense and intimidating.

The Goal

Move the program away from multiple google documents by designing a seamless and easy to use experience for web and mobile platforms.

Research

Studied LinkedIn Profinder and previous research by Peppercorn

The initial goal was to understand the platform and the content. Peppercorn had already done a lot of research on the market, competitors, and personas. I spent some time looking through what they had while also exploring other secondary research resources.

I also spent some time looking into LinkedIn Pro Finder to understand what clients were looking for when they choose a career coach.

Customer Mindsets

Confused but Open

“I am unsure about the next step in my career and am confused about where to start the job search process. There is too much information online.”


Looking for Something Specific

“I know what I want. I already have a resume and only need help networking and to find a job.”

Architecture

After a first pass through the document, I started organizing the content into different phases to separate it into clearly defined chunks with clear goals for the candidates to accomplish. This process and the next were done in collaboration with the creator of this program. We ended up with an overall architecture for the platform, future feature ideas, and an idea for how much and what to include in our MVP.

Wireframes

Following initial outlines I developed wireframes for the different kinds of pages that would be needed in the platform. This helped aid discussions around what would work best and discover potential usability issues. Our initial focus for the MVP was to develop Phase 1 and 2 of the product and the wireframes were focussed on them as well.



**Thanks to Anita Ganti who provided feedback and suggestions that helped me improve by questioning the design choices and content

Final Design

Once basic wireframes were approved and edited based on feedback from the team I started creating the final designs.



**Thanks to Micah who provided feedback and suggestions as a beta user and designer.

Content is presented as smaller, more digestible chunks rather than the pages of text that we had in the previous version. Used a clean layout with enough spacing between characters and lines to make reading easier.

Access to blog content is included where users have been found to get stuck most often and also to provide more information on frequently asked questions. Blogs are also an opportunity for Peppercorn to introduce their career coaches, who can add additional value to the program, to the user.

Phase 2 has been made easier by auto-filling the majority of the content from Phase 1 letting users move through this section quickly while also avoiding the need to go back and forth.

Learning

Learn to prioritize some and let go of others

In a startup there are too many things that need to be done but only so much time to do it. Pixel perfect designs are less important then ensuring the we have made the right UX decisions. Also a small startup team means that sometimes effective communication is faster and a better use of time than creating every single screen needed for the platform. I had to decide to leave some as wireframes and work with the developer later to audit the design if needed.


Focus on the MVP

It is easy to lose sight of the main goal if we are not clear about defining what needs to be done now and what can be left for later. We had to choose the pieces of the product that would provide the most value and set us apart from competitors.
