design research + interaction + food + diverse communities

"How might we reimagine common spaces to build diverse communities through food?"



duration

15 weeks


my role

research, ideation, and design concept development

approach

we re-examined the brief.

We started our process by examining the brief thoroughly to gain insights and understand the requirements followed by secondary research to gain inspiration.

initial decisions

chose community gardens.

We considered several community spaces but finally decided on community gardens because of:

interviews

information and ambience is key.

We conducted interviews with two different gardens - North Cass Community Garden and Nurturing Our Seeds. This helped us gain insight into the people and their motivations in both creating and making use of such spaces.

Main Insights:
1. People are interested in information about health and food.
2. Community gardens helps make the neighborhood cleaner and safer making people more likely to socialize.

experiences

explore. buy. cook.

We spent some time exploring markets with produce from these gardens, buying and cooking together to simulate what the experience could feel like.

framework

intriguing examples around us.

Community spaces require effort and engagement to be successful in bringing people together. A study on thriving community centers by Danny Spitzberg provided us a framework to follow in planning the event. Using this framework, we identified beneficial examples that we used to progress in our ideation.

With the framework in hand we ideated through multiple concepts and received feedback through post-it critiques

final design concept

event plan in four stages.

Stage 1: Spread the word to diverse groups - multi-generational, multi-cultural.

Stage 2: Interact with strangers and explore the garden.

Stage 3: Give everyone a role, even the smallest children. While adults prepare the ingredients, children can play with the recipe sheet.

Stage 4: Have fun, socialize, eat.
